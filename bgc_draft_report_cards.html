Draft Summary

The undisputed winners of the BGC draft room. Grifdy constructed the most talent-dense roster in the field — landing a former professional player, two Masters-tier DPS threats, and a high-Diamond support core — all while spending $24,000 under cap. The Uncs enter Summer 2026 as the clear favorites on paper, assuming Lava's pro pedigree translates to the tournament format and Grifdy's tank-first instincts carry over from the support role he's known for.

Tournament Outlook

"Grifdy built a championship roster at a discount. The Uncs have elite talent at every position that matters — tank, DPS, and support — and entered the tournament with $24k to spare. The central question isn't whether they're the best team on paper; it's whether Lava's transition from tank to DPS role and Grifdy's in-game leadership translates under match pressure. If it does, everyone else is playing for second."

Draft Summary

The Terabyte Titans have the worst draft efficiency in the field by a significant margin — spending $189,000 to acquire $126,000 in adjusted true value represents a $63,000 deficit, the largest in the tournament. Two catastrophic overpays at support define this draft: jhunli and Mmmmmyuh together cost $73,000 for players worth a combined $21,000. Aurum is a genuine bright spot, and the post-draft acquisition of jj183 via trade helps the DPS depth — but one GM carry and one smart trade cannot fully offset the damage done in the support slots.

Tournament Outlook

"The Terabyte Titans are the cautionary tale of the BGC Summer 2026 draft — a reminder that budget mismanagement at support can sink a roster regardless of how good your carry is. Aurum is legitimately elite and will put up numbers, and the post-draft trade landing jj183 is a genuine positive that sharpens the DPS line. But he cannot 1v2 the support deficit — jhunli and Mmmmmyuh collectively cost more than a Masters-tier acquisition would have while returning Bronze-to-Plat level value. A group stage exit is the most likely outcome unless Aurum's performances are genuinely historic."
